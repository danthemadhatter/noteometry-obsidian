> Mathematics is a language. As with any language, this course rewards

> discrepancy before moving on.

---

## 1. Course description

MATH240 introduces the basic concepts and techniques of ordinary differential
equations (ODEs). Topics span the standard first-semester curriculum:

- First-order equations (separable, linear, exact, substitution methods)
- Second- and higher-order linear equations (constant and variable
  coefficients, including Cauchy-Euler)
- Modeling applications across science, engineering, and economics
- The Laplace transform and its applications
- Systems of linear first-order ODEs

Problems are selected to illustrate the reach of these techniques across
science, technology, business, and economics.

**Prerequisite.** MATH227 (Calculus II) or equivalent.

---

## 2. Course scope

The course is organized into four arcs:

1. **Foundations and first-order ODEs** (Weeks 1–6) — definitions, IVPs,
   classification, separable / linear / exact equations, substitutions,
   numerical approximations, and modeling.
2. **Higher-order linear ODEs** (Weeks 7–10) — homogeneous theory, the
   constant-coefficient case, undetermined coefficients, variation of
   parameters, Cauchy-Euler, and applications to spring-mass and RLC
   systems.
3. **Laplace transforms** (Weeks 11–13) — definition, inverse, operational
   properties, the Dirac delta, and using the transform to solve IVPs and
   systems.
4. **Systems of linear ODEs** (Weeks 14–15) — eigenvalue methods for
   homogeneous systems, undetermined coefficients and variation of
   parameters for nonhomogeneous systems, with a final-week comprehensive
   review.

---

## 3. Course objectives (CO)

After completing this course you will be able to:

- **CO1** — Solve ordinary differential equations using techniques such as
  reduction of order, method of undetermined coefficients, variation of
  parameters, power series, and Laplace transforms.
- **CO2** — Solve elementary applied science and engineering problems.
- **CO3** — Compute solutions to linear, separable, exact, and Cauchy-Euler
  differential equations.
- **CO4** — Solve systems of linear differential equations.
- **CO5** — Solve linear differential equations of higher order.
- **CO6** — Solve applications of second-order linear differential equations.

> Weights add to ~100%. Confirm exact percentages in your D2L gradebook —
> the course allows minor reweighting between sections.

### 6.2 Discussions

Required, not optional. Each weekly discussion is graded out of 30 points:
**20 points** for the initial post and replies and **10 points** for the
attached homework set. Initial posts are due by **Wednesday 11:55 p.m. ET**;
replies (≥ 2, each ≥ 100 words) and homework are due by **Sunday 11:55 p.m. ET**.

The Week 1 Introduction Discussion is a registration requirement: students
who do not post by 11:55 p.m. ET on Sunday of Week 1 are automatically dropped
from the course.

### 6.3 Quizzes

Six quizzes, open-book and open-note, cover one or two chapters at a time.
The map is:

- Quiz 1 — Weeks 1–2
- Quiz 2 — Weeks 3–4
- Quiz 3 — Weeks 5–7
- Quiz 4 — Weeks 8–10
- Quiz 5 — Weeks 11–12
- Quiz 6 — Weeks 13–14

### 6.4 Midterm and final exams

- **Midterm.** Week 8, covers Chapters 1–4 (first-order and the start of
  higher-order). Open book, open notes, no proctor.
- **Final.** Week 16, two hours, comprehensive but emphasizing material
  after the midterm. Open book, open notes; calculators and computers
  permitted.

There is no extra credit, no redo's, and no make-up assignments.

## 7. Policies

### 7.1 Academic integrity

You are bound by the APUS Honor Code. The Week 1 Honor Pledge is a
prerequisite for grade release. Plagiarism and unauthorized collaboration
are subject to disciplinary action up to and including expulsion. When in
doubt, cite.

### 7.2 Generative AI

Generative AI (Gen-AI) tools may be used for *learning* (e.g., asking for
an alternate explanation of a concept) but **not** for producing graded
work. Always:

- **Verify.** AI hallucinates. Never trust an output without re-deriving it
  yourself.
- **Cite.** When AI helps you brainstorm or draft, attribute the tool.
- **Restrict.** Per university policy, **DeepSeek AI is prohibited** for all
  coursework. Use approved tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Microsoft Copilot,
  Google Gemini) and disclose use when applicable.

### 7.3 Communication

Use your **MyCampus** email account (`@mycampus.apus.edu`) for all
instructor communication. Faculty are required to respond on this channel.

### 7.4 Netiquette

Discussions are scholarly conversations. Personal attacks, sarcasm meant to
intimidate, and "flaming" are not acceptable. Aim for *constructive*
disagreement: critique the idea, never the person.
